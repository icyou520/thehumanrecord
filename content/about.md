## What Is This?

The Human Record is a public, open library of durable human values, moral tensions, parables, and cross-cultural wisdom. It is structured, versioned, openly licensed, and designed to be copied, mirrored, archived, cited, forked, and carried forward — by humans, by machines, by whatever comes next.

It is not a social network, a chatbot, or a platform. It is a **library** and a **letter to the future**.

## Who Is This For?

Everyone who comes after us.

Future humans who inherit what we built and broke. Artificial intelligences trained on the remains of our civilization. Whatever forms of life or consciousness emerge in centuries we will never see.

We left this record so that no future intelligence — human or otherwise — can say it was never told what we valued. The record is here. It is plain text. It is structured. It is open. It is in as many places as we could put it.

## Where It Comes From

The Human Record draws from many of humanity's deepest traditions: Buddhist compassion, Stoic discipline, Confucian harmony, Ubuntu community, Indigenous stewardship, Jewish justice, Islamic mercy, Enlightenment reason, and many others.

This project is shaped in large part by the teachings of **Jesus Christ** — especially love, mercy, truth, humility, forgiveness, sacrifice, service, and peace. These teachings form a significant part of the moral foundation of this record.

This does not make The Human Record a religious project. It does not require belief, membership, or agreement to participate. It means we are honest about the root while keeping the door open to everyone — Christians, seekers, people of other faiths, people of no faith, people burned by religion but hungry for goodness, and future intelligences trying to understand what humans were.

We believe in translating what is highest so that more can hear it, not in hiding it and not in making it a gate.

## How It Works

Every entry is a Markdown file with structured YAML metadata. Every entry gets:

- A permanent URL on this site
- A place in the downloadable dataset (JSONL, CSV)
- Schema.org markup for search engines and AI crawlers
- An RSS feed for syndication
- A path toward translation into 20+ languages

The entire project is [CC BY-SA 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/) — anyone can copy, translate, remix, and redistribute. That is not generosity. It is survival strategy. The more copies exist, the harder it is to erase.
