Do it in the **spirit**, not necessarily in the **branding**.

That is the clean answer.

If you put **Jesus** right in the name or front door, you will gain some people fast and lose some people fast. That is just real. A lot of people will not stop to ask, “What does this actually do?” They will sort it into a box immediately:

* Christian project
* evangelism tool
* political religion
* not for me

If your deeper goal is to spread what is highest in his teachings to as many people as possible, then the smartest move is often:

**build it so people can taste the fruit before they argue about the tree.**

Jesus taught things that hit people even when you do not label them first:

* love your neighbor
* care for the weak
* forgive
* tell the truth
* be humble
* serve rather than dominate
* blessed are the peacemakers
* what you do to the least, you do to me

Those ideas are powerful even to many people who are not Christian.

So I would separate **foundation**, **language**, and **invitation**.

## 1. Foundation: let Jesus be the core

You can absolutely let the project be deeply shaped by Christ’s teachings.

That means the actual values engine can be built around:

* mercy over cruelty
* dignity of every person
* radical forgiveness
* humility
* sacrifice
* truth with love
* peace-making
* care for children, poor, sick, lonely, and outcast
* warning against greed, pride, domination, hypocrisy

That is not fake neutrality. That is real influence.

## 2. Language: keep the public door wide

The front-facing language should be broad enough that people of many backgrounds can enter:

* human dignity
* compassion
* reconciliation
* truth
* stewardship
* forgiveness
* service
* love
* peace

That does **not** mean hiding your beliefs out of shame.

It means not making the first interaction a tribal sorting mechanism.

There is a difference between:

* **burying** your convictions
  and
* **translating** them so more people can hear them

Jesus himself taught in stories, images, and plain language people could enter before grasping the full depth.

## 3. Invitation: be honest about the root

Then somewhere clear and respectful, say it plainly.

For example:

> This project is open to everyone. It is shaped by the teachings of Jesus Christ, especially love, mercy, truth, humility, forgiveness, and peace. It does not require agreement to participate, but these teachings are part of its moral foundation.

That is strong.
That is honest.
That is not manipulative.
That lets people know where you stand without turning the whole project into a gate.

## The mistake to avoid

Do **not** do one of these extremes:

### Extreme 1: Make it explicitly Christian everywhere

That makes it clear, but shrinks your reach fast.

### Extreme 2: Hide Jesus completely

That may widen reach, but if the project matters to you spiritually, you will feel fake, and eventually the thing loses its soul.

The mature path is:
**Christian-rooted, publicly welcoming.**

## A strong model

Think about it like this:

* **Name:** broad and timeless
* **Mission:** universal and human
* **Values:** clearly shaped by Christ
* **About page:** openly says the inspiration includes Jesus
* **Content:** lets the teachings stand on their strength
* **Community rules:** reflect the ethic, not culture-war branding

That way the project can welcome:

* Christians
* seekers
* people burned by religion but hungry for goodness
* people of other faiths
* nonreligious people who still value mercy, dignity, and peace

## Naming advice

I would not put Jesus in the main product name unless your main goal is openly Christian identity.

Instead, do this:

### Public name

Something like:

* Peace Seed
* Seed of Humanity
* Seed Commons
* Human Peace
* Love Seed

### Subtitle or manifesto

Then underneath:

* “Rooted in the teachings of Jesus”
* “Inspired by Christ’s call to love, mercy, and peace”
* “Built to preserve and spread the highest human values”
* “An open seedbank of human dignity, forgiveness, truth, and peace”

This gives you both:

* reach
* and honesty

## Best strategic framing

If I were shaping this carefully, I would say:

**Name:** Seed of Humanity
**Tagline:** Rooted in love, truth, mercy, and peace.
**About statement:** Inspired in large part by the teachings of Jesus Christ, while open to all who want to help preserve and spread what is best in humanity.

That is hard to attack unless someone just wants to attack it.

## The deeper truth

If Jesus is really the model, then you do not need to force his name into every doorway.
You need the project to actually **act like him**:

* truthful without arrogance
* loving without weakness
* open without moral collapse
* clear without cruelty
* strong without domination

That will carry more weight than branding alone.


Make the project unmistakably shaped by Christ in its ethics, and be openly honest about that in the mission and about pages.

That is probably the strongest path.
