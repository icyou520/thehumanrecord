# Governance

The Human Record is a public commons. Its governance is designed to prevent capture by any single person, faction, ideology, or commercial interest.

## Principles

1. **No single owner.** The project belongs to its contributors and the public.
2. **Stewards, not rulers.** Maintainers are caretakers, not authorities.
3. **Transparency.** All editorial decisions are made in public (issues, pull requests, discussions).
4. **Anti-capture.** No political faction, religion, corporation, or individual may gain exclusive control.
5. **Forkability.** If governance fails, anyone can fork. The license guarantees this.

## Roles

### Contributors
Anyone who submits content, translations, corrections, or improvements via pull request.

### Reviewers
Trusted contributors who review and approve pull requests. Appointed by maintainers based on consistent, high-quality contributions.

### Maintainers
Stewards responsible for:
- Final approval of content merges
- Enforcing editorial standards
- Resolving disputes
- Managing releases and snapshots
- Protecting the project from capture

Maintainers serve rotating terms. No maintainer may serve indefinitely without reconfirmation by the other maintainers.

### Founder
The original creator has no permanent special authority. Once the project has three or more active maintainers, the founder becomes one maintainer among equals.

## Decision-Making

- **Content additions:** Require approval from at least one reviewer and one maintainer.
- **Content removals:** Require approval from at least two maintainers with a stated reason.
- **Schema changes:** Require approval from all active maintainers.
- **Governance changes:** Require approval from all active maintainers and a 14-day public comment period.

## Anti-Capture Rules

1. No maintainer may represent the same organization as another maintainer.
2. No single organization may employ or fund more than one-third of active maintainers.
3. The project may not accept funding that comes with editorial conditions.
4. If a hostile takeover is attempted (e.g., flooding with biased content, coordinated maintainer replacement), any maintainer may invoke a **governance freeze** — a 30-day period where no structural changes are permitted while the community deliberates.

## Dispute Resolution

1. Discuss in the relevant issue or pull request.
2. If unresolved, escalate to a maintainer vote.
3. If still unresolved, open a public governance discussion with a 14-day comment period.
4. The nuclear option is always available: fork the project. The license guarantees this right.

## Amendments

This governance document can be amended by unanimous maintainer approval after a 14-day public comment period. The amendment history is tracked in version control.
